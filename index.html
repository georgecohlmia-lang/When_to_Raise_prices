STEP THREE · THE CONVERSATION

Two pieces of language. The notice, and the pushback handler.

Most founders dread this conversation. In the lesson's experience, it almost never goes the way they fear — when the increase was telegraphed from the beginning and the communication is clear.

PIECE 01 · THE NOTICE
What you'll say to existing founding clients.

Most of your script is generated from the inputs you've already provided. We just need one piece in your voice: how you'll acknowledge what these founding clients have meant to the business.

"As a founding practice, you've been essential to getting us here — your early belief in this product is what made the next stage possible."

PIECE 02 · THE PUSHBACK HANDLER
What you'll offer if a client resists the increase.

Some clients will push back. The lesson is direct: don't walk the price back — that trains clients that pushback works, and every future increase becomes harder. Offer a transition period instead, with a clear end date.

A bridge from old price to new, with a hard end date. Shorter is safer.

A cap protects you. If everyone asks and you say yes to all, the transition becomes a permanent tier you didn't choose. Common: 25-50% of your founding base.

PIECE 03 · THE NEW-CLIENT FRAMING
How you'll describe the price to clients who arrive after the raise.

The framing shifts from "founding rate" to "price our product is worth." Honest about what's changed: you're not asking anyone to take a chance on something new — you're offering something with demonstrated results.

"Twelve practices using the product daily, clinical outcomes documentation showing 40% reduction in appointment time, and three case studies published this quarter."
